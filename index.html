Impact on Fertility Rates with Increase in the Cost of Living

A look into the relationship between the increasing
cost-of-living and the fertility rates in Greater Melbourne.

Australian Inflation Rate and the Victorian Fertility Rate

Over recent years, the cost-of-living has skyrocketed all around the world. Greater Melbourne is no stranger to this. With this spike in the cost-of-living, the economic pressures make it more difficult to provide for a family. The graph to the right illustrates the change in fertility rate, with inflation.

Key Terms

Fertility Rate:

The number of births per female in given population.

Inflation Rate:

The increase in prices over a year or other given time period.

Cost-of-living:

The prices of everyday commodities compared to the world average.

Influence of Average Rent Prices on Fertility Rates

With around 25% of Victorians renting their homes, the price of this necessity directly correlates with the fertility rate within Victoria's council areas. The higher the rent, the more difficult it is to afford to raise a family.
